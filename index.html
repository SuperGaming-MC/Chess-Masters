How to Play Chess

Basic Rules

Chess is played on an 8×8 grid with alternating dark and light squares. Each player begins with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. The goal is to checkmate your opponent's king.

Playing the Game

  1. White always moves first, and players take turns moving one piece at a time.
  2. To move a piece, click on it first and then click on its destination square.
  3. When you click on a piece, valid moves will be highlighted with green dots.
  4. If you're stuck, you can use the Hint button to get a suggested move.

How the Pieces Move

♟
Pawn

Moves forward one square, but captures diagonally. On its first move, a pawn can move two squares forward. Pawns promote to another piece when they reach the opposite end of the board.

♜
Rook

Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.

♞
Knight

Moves in an L-shape: two squares horizontally or vertically and then one square perpendicular to that direction. Knights can jump over other pieces.

♝
Bishop

Moves any number of squares diagonally.

♛
Queen

Combines the powers of the rook and bishop, moving any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

♚
King

Moves one square in any direction (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally). The king must be protected at all times from check.

About Chess Masters

Game History

Chess is one of the oldest and most popular board games in the world. It originated in northern India in the 6th century AD and spread to Persia. When the Arabs conquered Persia, chess was taken up by the Muslim world and subsequently spread to Southern Europe.
